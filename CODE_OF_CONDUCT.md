# Code of Conduct

quadstore is a small project. The code of conduct is correspondingly short.

## Be decent

Treat people the way you'd want to be treated. Critique code, not
people. Assume good faith. If you wouldn't say it to a colleague's
face in a meeting, don't put it in an issue.

## What gets you removed

- Personal attacks, harassment, slurs, or sustained hostility.
- Outing or doxxing other contributors.
- Spam, scams, or sustained off-topic posting.
- Repeated bad faith — claiming bugs to get attention, demanding
  unpaid work, sealioning maintainers.

If any of the above happens in an issue, PR, or any other channel
related to this project, we'll remove the offending content and the
person responsible. We don't have a complicated escalation tree
because we don't need one.

## What we promise

- We'll respond to issues and PRs in good faith.
- We'll be honest about what's in scope and what isn't (see
  [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md) for the scope of the project).
- We won't use the project as a platform for our own gripes.

## Enforcement

A maintainer's call. There's no committee, no anonymous reporting
mechanism, no formal process. If you have a concern, open a private
security advisory or email a maintainer (see [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md)
for the contact path) and we'll deal with it.

## Inspiration

Light edit of the [Recurse Center social rules](https://www.recurse.com/social-rules)
and the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/),
neither of which we're claiming as our own. If you've worked under
either, this should feel familiar.
